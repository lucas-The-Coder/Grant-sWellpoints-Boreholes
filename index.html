Cape Town Regulations

Compliance & Safety

Cape Town homeowners are regulation-aware post-drought. We provide installations that meet City of Cape Town requirements and guide you through every step.

  • ✔ Boreholes and wellpoints are classified as alternative water sources under the City of Cape Town Water By-law (2010, as amended 2018) and must be registered with the City after installation.
  • ✔ The City requires clear signage indicating non-potable water where applicable, particularly during restriction periods.
  • ✔ No cross-connection may exist between municipal drinking water and alternative water systems — we ensure correct, safe separation.
  • ✔ We provide practical guidance on signage, registration, and proper system identification — so your installation is compliant from day one.
  • ✔ All work is carried out in accordance with local plumbing practices and safe workmanship standards.

Installations must comply with the City of Cape Town Water By-law. Borehole and wellpoint water is classified as non-potable and must be clearly identified where required. This page provides general guidance only and does not constitute legal advice.
